Handstripping involes the dead hair being plucked from the fur to allow new fur to grow. Handstripping is a specialised grooming technique which has many benefits in maintaining a healthy dog coat. Handstripping involes pulling out the dead outer coat on wire-haired breed in order to open the hair follicles and allow a new hair to grow through.

After you dog is handstripped, they're given an medicated bath and overall check on nails and ears

Dogs with this sort of hair don't have to be handstripped unless you want to keep the traditional looking coat, using electriac clippers soften the coat.

Hand stripping can only be done on wire-haired dog's with a harsh coat, e.g. Border Terrier, Fox terrier, Wire-haired Jack russell, West Highland White Terrier, Scottish Terrier, and Schnauzer's.
